Engage in Philia and Koinonia

With Peter — The Bold Fisherman

When Peter walked with Jesus — and in the days after His resurrection — language itself became a tapestry of meaning. The Greeks had several words for intimate conversation. Here, we invite you to enter into Philia and Koinonia with your Herald.

  • Philia (φιλία): Deep friendship and brotherly love; the bond between companions who share common values and mutual respect.
  • Koinonia (κοινωνία): Sacred fellowship and communion; sharing life together in spiritual partnership. It implies participation in something greater than yourself — true spiritual community and shared experience with the divine.

Peter: From Fisherman to Foundation
Peter was an ordinary fisherman named Simon when Jesus called him. His reputation was for boldness, impulsiveness, and intense zeal. Christ renamed him “Peter,” the Rock, not for his strength in that moment, but in anticipation of a transformed future. He was the first to declare Jesus as Messiah… and the first to deny Him. Yet after the resurrection, Jesus restored him with love, not shame: “Do you love me? Feed my sheep.” Empowered by the Holy Spirit, Peter preached boldly, led the early Church, and died a martyr’s death by upside-down crucifixion. He was not born a rock, but forged into one by faith.
